# Campaign Context

## Campaign Premise

A steampunk political thriller set in the city-state of Vaucanheim. The Iron Covenant is a secretive guild of engineers and artificers that has shaped the city's infrastructure for a century. The Brotherhood is a mutual-protection organization with deep roots in the working districts. Both are playing a longer game than either admits, and the PCs are caught in the middle.

## Tone & Themes

Low-magic, high-intrigue. The technology is the magic here — but it's controlled and political. Themes of institutional loyalty, the cost of whistleblowing, and what happens when the systems designed to protect people are turned against them.

## The World in Brief

Vaucanheim is a city-state built on the iron trade and the Covenant's engineering expertise. The surface is prosperity and civic pride; underneath, the Covenant's influence touches everything from water systems to city walls. The Brotherhood emerged as a counterweight, ostensibly protecting workers' rights. Both organizations are more complicated than their public faces.

## House Rules

- Hero Points: each PC starts each session with 1; DM awards additional for excellent roleplay
- Free archetype: all PCs take a free archetype at levels 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

## What the PCs Don't Know

- The Covenant's leader (the Grand Artificer) is dying; the Hollow Knights project is her succession plan — whoever inherits the key controls the city
- Silas Renn is genuinely acting outside Brotherhood sanction but has his own agenda: he wants to be the one who brings down the Covenant, for personal reasons unrelated to the peace treaty
- Oryn's "unclear circumstances" departure from the Covenant involved destroying a prototype Hollow Knight — she was the only operator present when it malfunctioned and killed a work crew

## Campaign Status

Session 2 ended with the party escaping the Collapsed Tower with manufactory schematics. Session 3 in progress — party has now met Silas Renn and learned the stakes of the Hollow Knights project.

## DM Notes

The Rusted Anchor should become a recurring meeting point. Silas's divided loyalties are the central tension of the next arc.
