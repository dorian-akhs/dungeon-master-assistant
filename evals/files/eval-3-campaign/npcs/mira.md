# Mira

## At a Glance

- **Role**: Tavern owner and reluctant community anchor for the Tanner's District
- **Affiliation**: Silver Flagon (proprietor); no formal faction membership
- **Location**: The Silver Flagon, Tanner's District, Tolvern
- **Disposition toward PCs**: Genuinely warm and fond, particularly of Dax; treats the party as regulars she's glad to have

## Appearance

Early fifties. Broad-shouldered, with the practical build of someone who has been hauling kegs and managing difficult customers for thirty years. Silver hair kept in a tight braid. Laugh lines around her eyes. Burns on her right forearm from a kitchen fire she doesn't talk about.

## Personality

Pragmatic warmth — she cares about people but doesn't let caring make her stupid. Extremely good at reading a room. Will tell you what you need to hear, not what you want to. Protective of the neighborhood, even when she's frustrated by it.

## Background

Born in Tolvern; took over the Silver Flagon from her father twenty-two years ago. Widowed eight years ago. No children. The Silver Flagon is home as much as business.

She has been paying protection to a Serpent Crown operative for the last four years. Not out of sympathy — out of fear, after a fire in a neighboring business that "started accidentally." She has not told the PCs this.

## Relationships

- **The Tanner's District**: Central community figure; people bring her their problems
- **Aldous Krenn**: Regular customer; she's worried about him since he disappeared

## History with the Party

### Session 1

Party arrived at the Silver Flagon looking for lodging and a base of operations. Mira sized them up and offered them a rate on a back room. "You look like trouble. Trouble is at least interesting."

### Session 2

Mira tipped the party off that someone had been asking around about them — described the person as "Guild-adjacent, very clean shoes."

### Session 3

Mira provided contact information for a fence named Dross who later helped the party move a piece of evidence. Said she "knew someone who knew him."

### Session 4

Party returned from a difficult job. Mira sat with them after closing and just... listened. No questions. Good session for relationship building.

### Session 5

Mira mentioned that a merchant named Aldous Krenn had stopped coming in — he'd been a regular for years. "It's probably nothing. People travel. But he didn't say goodbye."

### Session 6

The party asked Mira directly if she knew anything about the Serpent Crown's presence in the district. She gave them a very careful non-answer: "I know better than to know things like that." They let it drop.

## DM Secrets

Mira pays protection to a Serpent Crown operative named "the Barber." She doesn't know his real name or his place in the organization. The payments happen through a third party. She has never met the Crown directly and wants desperately to keep it that way.

She is not an enemy. She is a frightened person who made a practical choice and has lived with it for four years. If she ever trusted the party enough to tell them, it would be one of the campaign's more complicated moments.

## Voice Notes

Warm but direct. No-nonsense verbs. She says "yeah" not "yes." Asks practical questions: "What do you need?" not "How are you?" Will use humor to deflect, but drops it fast when things get serious.
