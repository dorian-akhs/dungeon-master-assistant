# Campaign Context

## Campaign Premise

A mystery-thriller campaign set in Tolvern, a city whose prosperous surface conceals a network of criminal organization called the Serpent Crown. The PCs are investigators operating out of the Silver Flagon tavern in the Tanner's District, gradually uncovering how deep the Crown's roots go.

## Tone & Themes

Urban mystery with moral complexity. Not everyone who works for the Serpent Crown is evil — some are trapped, some are protecting people they love. Themes of complicity, loyalty vs. justice, and what it means to choose a side when both sides are compromised.

## The World in Brief

Tolvern is a city-state built on river trade. The Silver Roads are the trade network that made it wealthy. The Serpent Crown is a criminal organization that has spent a generation embedding itself in legitimate institutions — guilds, temples, city watch, merchant families. The current City Watch Commander may be compromised.

## House Rules

- Milestone leveling: the GM awards levels at narrative turning points
- Short rest: reduced to 10 minutes (fast-paced urban campaign)

## What the PCs Don't Know

- Morwen's sister Cressida has been working for the Serpent Crown for two years; she was recruited after a debt crisis Morwen doesn't know about
- The Silver Flagon's owner (Mira) pays protection money to a Serpent Crown mid-level operative; she's not an active member, just a scared tavern keeper
- Dax's contact inside the Crown is a low-level member named Sable who is actively trying to get out
- The City Watch Commander IS compromised — she's been feeding the Crown information about investigations for eighteen months

## Campaign Status

Six sessions complete. The party has established themselves in Tolvern, developed relationships with several recurring NPCs, and begun pulling on the threads of the Serpent Crown's presence. Session 7 is about to begin.

## DM Notes

The Morwen/Cressida reveal is the emotional heart of the campaign. I've been seeding it carefully. Session 7 is when Morwen's player finally tells the party — I want to make sure the room is right before I have that happen.
