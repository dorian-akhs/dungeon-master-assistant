# Sunken Archive

## Overview

A district of the old city never fully cleared when Tolvern built upward — part library, part ruin, home to scholars and monks who curate surviving records beneath the modern streets.

## Description

Lower passages open into spaces that feel older than the Merchant's Quarter architecture above. Shelving and scriptoria coexist with crumbling masonry. A small community maintains what survived the rebuild.

## Key Features

- Wandering stacks and scroll storage maintained by resident scholars
- Pell's workspace — densely packed scrolls and an improbably loaded desk

## Inhabitants

Scholars, monks, and archivists who tend the records; Pell is one prominent figure the party met.

## History

Formed from districts buried rather than demolished during Tolvern's vertical expansion.

## Connections

- **Merchant's Quarter / upper city**: Access routes link the undercity to commercial and civic zones above
- **Merchant's Hall Records Office**: Pell identified upper archives bureaucracy as the next obstacle
