# Campaign Context

## Campaign Premise

A city-based campaign set in Tolvern, a mercantile city-state strangled by the Merchants' Guild's grip on the Veilstone trade. The PCs are entangled in a conspiracy that connects the Ash Plague outbreak, a shadowy guild faction called the Black Ledger, and a thirty-year cover-up. The question at its heart: who gets to decide who survives?

## Tone & Themes

Dark political intrigue with moments of genuine warmth between the characters. Themes of institutional corruption, who pays the cost of progress, and the limits of loyalty. Not grimdark — people can do good, but the system fights back.

## The World in Brief

The Fractured Reach is a post-collapse world where the old empire shattered three centuries ago and city-states rose from the rubble. Tolvern is one of the wealthiest, built on the Veilstone trade — a mineral that inhibits Ash Plague spread. The Merchants' Guild controls the Veilstone supply. The Ash Plague still kills people in the outer districts.

## House Rules

- Milestone leveling: XP is not tracked; the GM awards level-ups at narrative turning points
- Inspiration: awarded by GM for excellent roleplay, risky choices, or moments of character truth; maximum 1 held at a time

## What the PCs Don't Know

- The Black Ledger has an agent inside the City Watch command structure
- Cassiel's mentor is alive but being held by the Guild as leverage
- Lyra's family didn't die randomly — they were in a district the Guild deliberately left unprotected to suppress a labor movement
- Kael's debt holder is a Black Ledger mid-level operative named "the Moth"

## Campaign Status

Session 1 complete. PCs have met Valdris Thornwood, established themselves as freelancers willing to operate outside official channels. They know the Guild is watching Thornwood and that something was taken from him. Session 2 just beginning.

## DM Notes

The Sunken Archive should feel genuinely old — I want it to contrast with the Guild's shiny new construction. Pell the Archivist is meant to be a recurring resource, someone who values information over money.
