# Session 002: Names in the Notebook

> **Quick Summary**: The party returned to Valdris Thornwood's apothecary on his summons, learned his Veilstone-independent plague formula was targeted in a storeroom break-in, and received a notebook of trusted contacts. They followed the trail to Sister Elara at the Temple of the Veil (Black Ledger pin sighting) and then to Pell the Archivist in the Sunken Archive, where a backup copy of the formula may still be recoverable — in exchange for a favor involving the Merchant's Hall Records Office.

---

## Events

### Merchant's Quarter — Approach and Thornwood's Apothecary

The party entered the Merchant's Quarter in early morning fog. Kael kept watch for tails and noticed a gray-cloaked figure half a block back who slipped into an alley when observed — significance uncertain.

At Valdris Thornwood's shop, Thornwood admitted someone had broken into his storeroom two nights prior. He described the theft as a formula from his research: a method for treating the Ash Plague without Veilstone. He believes the Merchants' Guild (and its surveillance of him) is responsible, and fears the work will be monetized while people die. He gave the party a leather notebook listing three trusted contacts. In dialogue, he stated he grew up in Ironhold and is familiar with Guild operations from that experience.

Thornwood named Sister Elara (Temple of the Veil) as the first contact — she had previously supplied a plague sample that changed his understanding of transmission.

Lyra examined the notebook carefully (Investigation); three names and addresses were visible with nothing obviously hidden to a casual inspection.

### Temple of the Veil — Reliquary with Sister Elara

The party traveled to the Scholar's District temple. Kael watched the entrance while Cassiel and Lyra were directed to the reliquary. Sister Elara, gray-haired and calm, confirmed she had been expecting Thornwood's people.

Elara reported seeing a figure leave via the alley behind Thornwood's shop two nights prior, carrying something wrapped in oilcloth. She did not see a face. She did see a pin: a Merchants' Guild sigil, but not the standard merchant chapter — she named the Black Ledger and treated the name as serious.

### Sunken Archive — Pell the Archivist

Following the notebook, the party descended into the undercity region known as the Sunken Archive — part library, part ruin, with a small scholar community maintaining old records. They met Pell, an elderly gnome archivist. Pell confirmed Thornwood had sent them and that he had been holding a copy of the formula as Thornwood's insurance against Guild seizure.

Pell offered the copy on condition the party retrieve something for him from the upper archives: the Merchant's Hall Records Office, where his access has been blocked (he claims illegally) for three months. He stated those records contain evidence the Black Ledger has operated inside Tolvern for at least thirty years — older than the current Guild Master's predecessors.

The session ended before any infiltration of the Records Office.
